To AI or not to AI - is that a question?

Here come my two cents. Cent no1: No, it's not a question. Cent no2: If we look at the amount of resources literally wasted by AI use, we really shouldn't have to ask that question - if anything then rhetorically.

To me, AI seems to have become this gimmick or shiny new toy even, many people use just for the sake of using it. There surely are very useful areas of application (medicine, automation, ..). Yet, I don't understand why using our own brain power is increasingly getting out of fashion. Shouldn't AI and new technological advances in general, help saving time for more human-centred space? Right now, it looks more like it helps turning all the brain capacity into mush by training humans to rely on tech while suggesting the trade off to be either "AI" or "you think for yourself". In my humble opinion, though, the trade off should rather look like AI may help us train our brains and ease the heavy lifts, so we can focus on what's more urgent. Wouldn't it be nobler to take arms against a see of troubles and by opposing, end [the slings and arrows of outrageous fortune]?


A stretched little literature deepdive on audio processing

A few years ago, I studied speech technology. However, life came in the way and I don't remember much of it. Just buzzwords like fourier transform, phoneme, amplitude or fundamental frequency. And i think forgetting is sad. So I decided to re-study some of the material and save my study notes here. For future reference.
These will be my sources:

Handbook of Acoustics Seventh Edition, F. Alton Everest & Ken Pohlmann (2022)
Introduction to Speech Processing
Bäckström, Okko Räsänen, Abraham Zewoudie, Pablo Pérez Zarazaga, Liisa Koivusalo, Sneha Das, Esteban Gómez Mellado, Mariem Bouafif Mansali, Daniel Ramos, Sudarsana Kadiri, Paavo Alku, and Mohammad Hassan Vali “Introduction to Speech Processing”, 2nd Edition, 2022.
